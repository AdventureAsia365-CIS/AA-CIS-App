# Toolkit: Psychology + NLP Copywriting + Pricing Strategy

This toolkit supports the formulas in the reference folder. Use these tools carefully and ethically. The goal is not manipulation; the goal is clearer persuasion, stronger relevance, and lower decision friction.

## Psychology + NLP Copywriting Toolkit

### A. 18 psychological effects and cognitive biases

1. **Loss Aversion** — people feel losses more strongly than equivalent gains. Use to show the cost of inaction.
2. **Scarcity** — limited quantity, timing, or access can increase perceived value when scarcity is real.
3. **FOMO** — fear of missing out works when the opportunity is relevant and time-sensitive.
4. **Social Proof** — people trust what others have chosen, reviewed, or recommended.
5. **Authority** — expertise, credentials, data, and institutional trust reduce doubt.
6. **Specificity Bias** — specific numbers, dates, names, and details feel more believable than vague claims.
7. **Anchoring** — the first number or comparison influences perceived value.
8. **Contrast Effect** — the value of an option becomes clearer when compared with an alternative.
9. **Endowment Effect** — people value something more when they imagine owning or experiencing it.
10. **Commitment and Consistency** — small agreements make larger actions feel more natural.
11. **Reciprocity** — useful value given first increases willingness to respond.
12. **Halo Effect** — one strong credibility signal can improve perception of the whole brand.
13. **Cognitive Fluency** — easy-to-read messages feel more true and trustworthy.
14. **Zeigarnik Effect** — unfinished loops hold attention.
15. **Peak-End Rule** — people remember emotional peaks and endings more than the full experience.
16. **Identifiable Victim Effect** — one specific person or case is more emotionally compelling than a vague group.
17. **Social Currency** — people share content that helps them look smart, helpful, funny, early, or bold.
18. **Risk Reversal** — guarantees, trials, demos, and transparent processes reduce purchase anxiety.

### B. 10 NLP copywriting techniques

1. **Open Loop** — start a thought that the reader wants completed.
2. **Presupposition** — phrase the sentence as if a useful belief is already true.
3. **Embedded Command** — include a clear action phrase inside natural language.
4. **Sensory Language** — use sight, sound, touch, taste, smell, or bodily feeling.
5. **Future Pacing** — help the reader imagine the desired future state.
6. **Mirroring** — use the audience’s own language and situation.
7. **Reframing** — change how the reader interprets a problem or opportunity.
8. **Identity Labeling** — name the type of person the message is for.
9. **Double Bind** — offer two choices that both move the reader forward. Use ethically.
10. **Power Words** — use emotionally charged but appropriate words. Avoid overhype.

### C. Pricing strategy and psychological anchors

1. **Anchor Price** — show a reference point before the actual offer to frame value.
2. **Decoy Option** — include a comparison option that makes the best-value package easier to choose.
3. **Good-Better-Best** — create three tiers for different customer needs.
4. **Price Framing** — express price by day, use, outcome, or avoided cost when helpful.
5. **Value Stack** — list included components before showing price.
6. **Risk Reversal** — pair pricing with guarantee, trial, support, or transparent cancellation.
7. **Urgency Window** — use real deadlines for launch pricing, early access, or event-based offers.
8. **Bonus Framing** — bonuses should remove objections, not clutter the offer.

## Ethical use rules

- Do not create fake scarcity.
- Do not overclaim health, finance, or performance results.
- Do not use fear without giving a real solution.
- Do not imply guarantees unless they exist.
- Do not hide important conditions.
- Use persuasion to clarify value, not to pressure people into bad decisions.
