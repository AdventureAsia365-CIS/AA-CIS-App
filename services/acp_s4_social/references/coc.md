# COC (Content of Content): Content inside content

COC is a repurposing system. One strong pillar asset becomes many smaller assets for different channels. It helps teams save time, maintain message consistency, and extract more value from deep content.

## 5 persuasive COC models

### 1. Blog/Article Pillar → Micro-Contents

Turn a 1,500–3,000 word article into social posts, quotes, carousel points, email snippets, short videos, and FAQs.

### 2. Video Pillar → Multi-Format

Turn a 10–20 minute video into clips, captions, posts, newsletters, carousels, and scripts.

### 3. Podcast Pillar → Audiogram + Text

Turn a 30–60 minute podcast into audiograms, quotes, show notes, LinkedIn posts, email summaries, and article ideas.

### 4. Live/Webinar Pillar → Evergreen Assets

Turn a live session into replay content, Q&A posts, clips, email sequences, sales assets, and evergreen guides.

### 5. Case Study Pillar → Multi-Touchpoint

Turn a detailed case study into sales proof, testimonials, before/after posts, problem-solution posts, landing page proof, and email nurture.

## 7 core techniques in COC

### 1. Pillar Quality Above All

Weak pillar content creates weak derivatives. The original asset must contain strong ideas, proof, structure, and reusable angles.

### 2. Section-to-Asset Mapping

Map each section of the pillar to a smaller asset: hook, insight, proof, quote, framework, objection, CTA, or story.

### 3. Format Adaptation

Do not copy-paste. Adapt the same idea to the channel: LinkedIn post, Facebook caption, TikTok script, email, carousel, or landing section.

### 4. Frequency Optimization

Spread derivative content across channels without overwhelming the audience.

### 5. Cross-Linking

Connect micro-content back to the pillar asset or next step: article, report, product page, signup, or consultation.

### 6. Temporal Spacing

Publish derivatives over time. Do not release everything at once.

### 7. Refresh and Re-purpose

Update older pillar assets with new examples, data, headlines, and channel formats.

## 8-point checklist before publishing a COC plan

1. Is the pillar asset strong enough?
2. Are the main ideas clearly extracted?
3. Is each derivative adapted to its channel?
4. Is there a publishing rhythm?
5. Are CTAs mapped properly?
6. Is there internal linking or cross-linking?
7. Is the content varied enough?
8. Is there a refresh plan?

## Mistakes to avoid

1. Turning weak content into many weak pieces.
2. Copy-pasting the same text everywhere.
3. Ignoring channel behavior.
4. Publishing too much at once.
5. No CTA or link structure.
6. Losing the original message.

## Recommended psychology

Mere Exposure Effect, Consistency, Authority, Social Proof, Familiarity, and Message Reinforcement.

## Recommended NLP

Reframing, repetition with variation, open loops across posts, and channel-specific phrasing.
