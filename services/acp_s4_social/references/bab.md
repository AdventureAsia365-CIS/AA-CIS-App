# BAB (Before → After → Bridge)

BAB shows transformation. It begins with the current unsatisfactory state, paints the desired future state, then introduces the bridge that gets the customer there. BAB works because people buy movement: from confusion to clarity, pain to relief, ordinary to better.

## 5 persuasive BAB models

### 1. Classic BAB (clear transformation, retargeting)

Structure: Before → After → Bridge.

Use when the audience already understands the problem and wants a better state.

### 2. BAB Extended Journey

Use for longer content where the transformation needs more proof, context, and emotional build-up.

### 3. BAB with Witness

A third person tells the transformation story. Use for testimonials, case studies, and social proof.

### 4. Reverse BAB (After → Before → Bridge)

Start with the result, then reveal the old situation and the bridge. Use when the outcome is strong enough to hook attention.

### 5. BAB Triple-Sensory

Use rich sensory contrast between before and after. Strong for wellness, beauty, travel, food, lifestyle, and experience-based offers.

## 7 core techniques in BAB

### 1. Mirror Hook (Before)

Open with a situation the reader recognizes. They should feel: “That sounds like me.”

### 2. Sensory Contrast

Make Before and After feel different through specific details. Contrast creates emotional movement.

### 3. Awakening Moment

Show the moment when the character realizes the old way is no longer enough. This creates narrative tension.

### 4. Detailed Future Pacing (After)

Paint the desired future with time, place, action, and feeling. Make the after-state concrete.

### 5. Bridge as Tool

The bridge is the product, method, system, service, or process. Present it as the practical mechanism that makes the after-state possible.

### 6. Identifiable Witness

When using testimonials, make the person specific enough to feel real: role, situation, context, timeline, or measurable result.

### 7. Bridge Risk Reversal

Reduce fear around crossing the bridge. Use trial, guarantee, process clarity, support, or proof.

## 8-point checklist before publishing BAB

1. Is the Before state specific and recognizable?
2. Is the After state concrete, not vague?
3. Is the contrast emotionally clear?
4. Is the bridge believable?
5. Is the product presented as a mechanism, not magic?
6. Is there proof or a witness when needed?
7. Does the CTA invite the reader to take the first step?
8. Does the tone avoid overpromising?

## Mistakes to avoid

1. Making Before too generic.
2. Making After unrealistic or exaggerated.
3. Skipping the bridge mechanism.
4. Turning the story into a claim without proof.
5. Overusing transformation language without showing real change.

## Recommended psychology

Contrast Effect, Future Pacing, Endowment Effect, Social Proof, Loss Aversion, and Risk Reversal.

## Recommended NLP

Mirroring, sensory contrast, embedded commands, identity labeling, and future pacing.
