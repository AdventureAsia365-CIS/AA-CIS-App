# SLAP (Stop → Look → Act → Purchase)

SLAP is built for fast attention and rapid movement. It stops the scroll, makes the reader look deeper, asks for action, and moves toward purchase. It is useful for short ads, TikTok/Reels, punchy social posts, and visual-first content.

## 5 persuasive SLAP models

### 1. SLAP Shock Number

Use a surprising number to stop attention. The number must be relevant and credible.

### 2. SLAP Forbidden Statement

Use a reverse or “do not do this” statement to trigger curiosity. Avoid manipulative fear.

### 3. SLAP Contrarian Hook

Open with a belief-challenging statement. Useful when the market repeats the same advice.

### 4. SLAP Myth-Busting

Start by breaking a common myth, then explain the better view.

### 5. SLAP Visual Pattern Break

Pair the copy with an unexpected image, layout, or first frame. Strong for video and creative ads.

## 7 core techniques in SLAP

### 1. Pattern Interrupt Hook (Stop)

Break the normal feed pattern with a surprising line, visual, question, or contradiction.

### 2. Curiosity Loop with Zeigarnik Effect

Open a loop the reader wants to complete. Unfinished information keeps attention.

### 3. Specificity Bias (Look)

Use specific numbers, details, or examples so the claim feels real.

### 4. Authority Drop (Look)

Add one fast credibility signal: expert, data source, years of experience, client result, or recognized reference.

### 5. Value Reveal Pacing

Do not reveal everything at once. Pace the value so the reader continues.

### 6. Soft Identity Labeling (Act)

Invite action by naming the type of person who should act.

### 7. Urgency Stack with FOMO (Purchase)

Use urgency ethically: deadline, limited batch, event date, seasonal timing, or early-access window.

## 8-point checklist before publishing SLAP

1. Does the first line or visual stop attention?
2. Is the curiosity loop clear?
3. Is the claim specific?
4. Is there a credibility signal?
5. Does the content move quickly?
6. Is the action simple?
7. Is urgency real, not fake?
8. Does the purchase step feel natural?

## Mistakes to avoid

1. Shock without substance.
2. Clickbait that does not deliver.
3. Too much explanation.
4. Fake urgency.
5. Asking for purchase before value is clear.

## Recommended psychology

Pattern Interrupt, Zeigarnik Effect, Curiosity Gap, Specificity Bias, Authority, FOMO, and Loss Aversion.

## Recommended NLP

Open loops, contrarian statements, embedded commands, identity labels, and short action verbs.
